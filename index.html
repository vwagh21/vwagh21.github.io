About

I’m a second-year Master’s student in Robotics at Carnegie Mellon University, working with Dr. Inseung Kang in the MetaMobility Lab. My research focuses on personalized exoskeleton control using LLMs in the loop and on edge, as well as temporal ML and transfer learning for real-time, continuous user adaptation.

Before CMU, I was a MITACS intern working with Dr. Sarah Kraeutner at the University of British Columbia and a Financial Analyst at Deloitte. I completed my Bachelor’s in Mechanical Engineering at COEP Tech University in Pune, India.

I have experience spanning ML, CV, mechatronics, and edge deployment, and I’m broadly interested in ML, AI and robotics technologies that meaningfully improve lives.

I am seeking full-time opportunities in ML, AI, robotics, or research engineering starting May 2026.

Research

LLM-Guided Adaptive Control for Exoskeletons Using Real-Time User Speech

SLMs, Phi2, Whisper-tiny, Jetson Orin Nano

How can we build natural language interfaces that can map qualitative user feedback to quantitative changes in control frameworks to bring about real-time personalization?

Personalized torque prediction for stroke populations

Transfer learning, Temporal ML, Deep learning, TCNs

How can we enable accurate torque estimation for clinical (stroke) populations without large-scale training datasets and heterogenous gaits across subjects?

Evaluating MediaPipe for post-stroke assessment

MediaPipe, R, Matlab, Kinematics

Can off-the-shelf, easy to deploy toolkits assist or replace subjective assessments of stroke recovery?

Evaluating MediaPipe for fine upper limb motion capture

MediaPipe, R, Matlab, Python

Are off-the-shelf, easy to deploy CV toolkits accurate enough for tracking fast and dextrous movements of fine limbs?

Projects

VLM-based Human-Robot Voice Interface – Talking to Robots

SLMs, Phi4, SFT, Jetson Orin Nano

Developed a human-robot interface using speech and vision inputs to finetune a mini VLM for long-horizon planning and execution of user-personalized lower limb exoskeleton control.

Automated IV Insertion Bot – Medical Robotics

Autodesk, CAD, 3D Printing, Electronics, Control

Designed linear-actuation mechanisms for needle insertion, integrating Arduino control, computer vision-based vein localization, and arm control.

Generative Adversarial Imitation Learning – Intro to Robot Learning

Inverse reinforcement learning, GAIL

Applied inverse reinforcement learning with GAN-based reward models to simulate human locomotion, exploring state-level and trajectory-level GAIL approaches.

LQR for humanoid balancing – Optimal Control and Reinforcement Learning

MuJoCo, LQR

Designed an LQR-based controller to stabilize a human model balancing on a dynamically moving platform in MuJoCo.
